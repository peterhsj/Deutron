About Deutron

Founded in July, 2002 by Dr. Frank Huang, founder of PSC (Powerchip Technology Corp.), Deutron commits to provide the cutting-edge memory solution to help customers enriching their digital life. Dedicated to professionalism and innovation, we sincerely have cultivated our brand, “MIRA”, for many years and established good track record in worldwide memory business with extensive product portfolios include Consumer DRAM,Consumer Flash , MCP etc., Deutron targets herself as an expert of memory solution and component solution.

Deutron has always been a customer-driven company, and focus her efforts on providing the highest quality products with attentive after sales service and support to ensure total customer satisfaction. Deutron’s strong commitment to quality requires performing at the highest standards - they relate to ID design, raw materials purchasing and supply-chain management, manufacturing processes, quality control and complete testing.

Our strong commitment to quality will drive us to provide innovative products with superior quality. It keeps us at the forefront of our industry and leaves the competition far behind.

Milestones

  • 1994 PSC was established at Hsinchu Science Park.
  • 1996 PSC DRAM mass production started (OEM/Foundry Fab for Mitsubishi).
  • 1996 MIRA company was established to sell “MIRA” brand DRAM module and consumer DRAM.
  • 2002 1’st 12" fab mass production, collaborated with Elpida Japan.
  • 2002 MIRA renamed as Deutron focus on DRAM spot market.
  • 2003 PSC started NAND Flash R&D.
  • 2005 PSC launched 1st NAND Flash – AG-AND 4Gb MLC.
  • 2005 Deutron start marketing AG-AND Flash to spot market.
  • 2009 PSC launched 70nm 4Gb NAND Flash.
  • 2011 May PSC changed business model to foundry.
  • 2011 PSC change name to PTC.
  • 2011 PTC launched 40nm 16Gb NAND Flash.
  • 2011 PTC launched 1’st MCP product, 1Gb SLC NAND + 512Mb LP DDR1.
  • 2011 Deutron extend MCP product.
  • 2012 Deutron launched 30nm 4Gb DDR3.
  • 2012 Deutron launched 1’st SPI NAND Flash, 40nm 512 Mb.
  • 2013 Deutron launched 40nm 1Gb PPI NAND Flash.
  • 2014 Deutron launched 40nm 2/4Gb PPI NAND Flash.
  • 2015 Deutron launched 40nm 1Gb SPI NAND Flash.
  • 2015 Deutron launched 30nm 2Gb DDR3 (1866MHz).
  • 2017 Deutron launched 25nm 4Gb DDR3.
